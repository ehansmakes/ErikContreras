Blog Post 2 - Article Breakdown

November 8th, 2020

Why did you choose the elements you used and how do they help describe the structure of the content?

I chose the elements that I used for my blog’s design by trying to mimic the structure of a newspaper. This gave me a page with multiple “article” elements inside the “main” element. After reading: “HTML5 Semantic Tags: What Are They and How To Use Them!”, it is good to know that search engines work best when a webpage uses elements correctly!

What are you experimenting with and what kind of look are you trying to achieve?

Regarding the aesthetics of my blog’s design, I have been trying to emulate the look of the old internet [mid 90’s to early 00’s]. While I feel that web design from this era wasn’t the best, such as the tiled backgrounds and clashing colors found in GeoCities, I feel these odd design choices create a lot of webpages with personality. I am hoping to bring back these retro aesthetics with a modern approach to webpage design. I would like my blog to be like an ugly sweater for the internet.

What is most challenging in terms of the visual design?

The biggest challenge in my blog design is with how to incorporate scroll bars for my content in a way that doesn’t hide the core elements of my content. I am also going to try to add fun “easter eggs” around the site, but I hope it doesn’t come across as annoying or distracting from the main content. For example, I am wondering if there is a tasteful way to have MIDI music play on my webpage.
